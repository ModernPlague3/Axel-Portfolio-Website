Designing B.R.U.N.O

Below is a draft technical report structured to highlight my design approach, engineering decisions, and future roadmap.

1. Project Overview

What is B.R.U.N.O?

B.R.U.N.O is an avionics computer aimed at reimagining and uplifting avionics systems used in rocketry. It was named as a tribute to an industry tradition I encountered at Amazon Kuiper Systems, where avionics computers were named after the designer's dog.

Problem Statement & Significance

  • Previous systems suffered from limited expandability and poor signal integrity.
  • Inadequate power sourcing and lack of redundancy compromised telemetry during critical phases.
  • B.R.U.N.O resolves these issues with higher power handling, dual-microcontroller redundancy, and expanded sensor integration.

2. System Architecture & Design

Core Components

  • Power Subsystem: Utilizes a buck-boost converter for stable 5V power and an LDO for 3.3V regulation.
  • Processing Units: STM32F5 handles navigation, while STM32H7 executes flight control algorithms.
  • Sensor Suite: Includes IMU, magnetometer, temperature, and absolute pressure sensors.

Design Principles & Standards

  • Redundant B.R.U.N.O units ensure system integrity via UART heartbeat communication.
  • RF design follows 50-ohm impedance matching principles to reduce noise.
  • Protection includes TVS diodes for electrostatic discharge protection.

3. Power & Communication Systems

Power Strategy

  • Battery system supports up to 26V input.
  • High-efficiency buck-boost converter achieves ≈90% efficiency.

Communication Protocols

  • UART for inter-microcontroller communication.
  • SPI for high-speed LoRa transmission.
  • I2C for low-speed sensor interfacing.

4. Sensors & Actuators

Sensor Calibration & Testing

  • IMU and magnetometer used for real-time navigation.
  • Pressure sensor calibration achieved using MATLAB scripts and a reference PSI tool.
  • Automated servo actuation at 100 PSI confirms apogee detection.

5. Testing & Validation

Ongoing tests include:

  • Bench testing of power regulators and signal integrity.
  • Environmental testing with simulated temperature cycling and vibrations.

6. Future Roadmap

  • Integration of real-time operating system (RTOS) for enhanced data processing.
  • Development of a multi-board PCBA stack-up with dedicated Power Management (PMAD) and Relay Boards.
  • Full alignment with aerospace standards (RTCA DO-254, MIL-STD-461).

7. Conclusion

B.R.U.N.O is a significant leap forward in avionics design, incorporating real-time flight control, redundancy, and aerospace-grade fault tolerance. With planned software and hardware upgrades, this project has the potential to set a new benchmark in rocketry avionics.

J.A.X.O.N Motor and Servo AI Driver

J.A.X.O.N motor and servo AI Driver is an ultra-low cost, high-performance control system leveraging the ESP32 to deliver precise motor and servo control. Utilizing encoder data and powered by a USB-C PD IC for streamlined power management, J.A.X.O.N offers versatile connectivity via serial and I2C—enabling seamless integration with edge AI modules like the Jetson Nano. Built from readily available modules, this innovative solution combines autonomous control capabilities with advanced communication for cutting-edge applications.
